What is The Chroma Incident?

Premise:

The Achromat, a sentient hive mind virus of alien origin, has devoured all the color in the world

Power your BRG (Big Rainbow Gun) with prismatic beacons of varying abilities, vanquish the achromat minions, restore color and save the world.

I imagine The Achromat as visually inspired by a blend between the mimics in Edge of Tomorrow...

...and the stealthbots from Mitchells vs the machines.

Achromat Enforcer Concept

The Achromat are geometric and angular in appearance, but very non-human in form

Player Concept

Our Hero is in a space suit weilding the BRG. Inspired by 80s sci-fi and Turbo Kid. Open to ideas for what they should look like

Look And Feel

  • Resolution: 640x360

  • no palette restrictions, no strict pixel-style rules
  • retro-futuristic UI
  • possibility of pre-rendered 3D sprites
  • normal-mapped art for cool 2d lighting effects

Stuff I'd welcome help with

Art

  • character art: various Achromat goon enemies, multi-part boss sprites
  • environment art: high tech panels, lots of greebles, think space station, lab
  • weapon art: wild looking space guns, colorful projectiles

Code and design

  • level design: game will have a mix of procedural generation and pre-designed areas
  • puzzle design: specific combinations of weapon effects needed to progress
    • `
  • particle effects: many different weapon effects needed with unique appearances
  • enemy design and enemy behavior: different size and types planned that need unique behaviors

Music

  • Electronic / synthwave style music
  • Title screen track
  • in-game combat track
  • boss / danger action track

Sounds

  • Real recorded sounds welcome, not lofi/chip
  • various machine Sounds: fans, clanks, mechanisms opening/closing
  • various weapon sounds: lasers, fire, explosions, booms
  • various enemy sounds: deep robotic distorted howls and barks, glitch sounds
  • various UI sounds: computer chimes, dings, beeps

My priorities for this game:

  • fun and challenging combat
  • deeper player engagement than my previous games
  • A good blend of procgen and level design
  • become proficient in godot for 2d games
